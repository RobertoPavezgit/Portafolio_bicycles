Within the three months studied in Figure, it can be observed that the time of day when bicycles are most used for transportation is during the afternoon (12 am - 7 pm), with 181,989 trips, representing 51.25% of the total trips during January, February, and March 2019. This figure is quite high, so it will be important to focus on improvements and incentives within this time zone where peak demand occurs. On the other hand, the second most frequented time is in the mornings, which could be common for people who commute to work by bicycle or users who exercise in the morning. These trips account for a total of 140,462 journeys, which is around 39.56% of the trips within the three months studied.

Finally, the least frequented time by users is at night (around 7 pm and before 6 am), with only 32,618 trips, equivalent to 9.19%. This percentage is low and should be studied more precisely to understand the reasons and implement new measures to increase traffic during this time. This could be due to safety or work-related issues, so surveying users during this period would be appropriate. This point will be included in the recommendations to guide marketing strategies to understand and improve the low use of bicycles as a means of transport at night. It is also important to note that within the bar chart, the orange segment represents users with an annual subscription or membership, while the blue segments represent occasional users or customers. Within this segment, it is clear that the number of users with subscriptions is much higher than the number of occasional customers across all three time periods. Although the number of users with memberships is high, there is still 6.34%, or 23,163 users, who could be integrated through annual subscriptions or memberships.
